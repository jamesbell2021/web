James Bell

I am a Lecturer in Games Design and Interactive Development with a strong passion for creating meaningful, engaging digital experiences. My work sits at the intersection of creativity, technology, and problem‑solving — where code becomes play.

About Me

My background spans game design theory, technical implementation, and creative experimentation. I specialise in helping learners understand how systems work beneath the surface — from gameplay mechanics and level design to scripting, data‑driven logic, and real‑time interaction.

I believe the best games are not just visually impressive, but designed with intent: every mechanic teaches the player something, every system supports the experience, and every choice has meaning.

Passion for Games Design

Games design, for me, is about more than entertainment. It is a powerful medium for storytelling, learning, experimentation, and self‑expression. I am particularly interested in:

• Interactive systems and player agency
• Gameplay mechanics and systemic design
• Prototyping using engines such as Unreal Engine
• Technical problem solving through creative coding
• Teaching design thinking through play

Whether developing prototypes, mentoring students, or exploring new technologies, my focus is always on bridging the gap between creative vision and technical execution.
