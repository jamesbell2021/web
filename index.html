Level 3 Games Development – Course Overview

The computer games industry is one of the fastest‑growing creative sectors globally, offering careers across PC, console, mobile, VR, and AR platforms. At Belfast Met, students follow one of two specialist pathways depending on their interests and strengths.

Castlereagh Campus – Game Development Pathway

This pathway focuses on the technical and interactive aspects of game production. Students develop the skills required to design, build, and programme playable game experiences.

  • Unreal Engine mastery and Blueprint scripting
  • Gameplay mechanics and technical systems design
  • 2D, 3D, VR, and AR experience development
  • Game audio creation, including sound design and implementation
  • Project‑based studio practice and problem solving

Industry‑standard tools are used throughout the course, including Unreal Engine, Autodesk software, Blender, ZBrush, Adobe Creative Suite, and professional audio tools.

Titanic Quarter Campus – Game Art & Design Pathway

The Titanic Quarter pathway is ideal for students with a strong interest in creativity and visual storytelling, focusing on the artistic side of game development.

  • Concept art and visual development
  • 2D and 3D modelling, animation, and environments
  • User interface (UI) and UX design
  • Professional portfolio development

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching approach is centred on learning through making. I emphasise practical development, iteration, and experimentation, helping students understand not only how tools work, but why design decisions matter.

I aim to create an inclusive, supportive learning environment that mirrors industry practice and develops confident, independent, and creative problem solvers.
